About Dresden

Dresden is the capital city of the German state of Saxony. It is the 12th most populous city.
The city was known as the Jewel Box, because of its baroque and rococo city centre.
Since 1990, Dresden has again become a cultural, educational and political centre of Germany.
The Dresden University of Technology is part of the German Universities Excellence Initiative.
Dresden is one of the most visited cities in Germany with 4.7 million overnight stays per year.
Its most prominent building is the Frauenkirche located at the Neumarkt, built in the 18th century.
Other famous landmarks include the Zwinger, the Semperoper and the Dresden Castle.
The city is home to the renowned Dresden State Art Collections, originating from the Saxon electors in the 16th century.
Dresden's Striezelmarkt is considered the first genuine Christmas market in the world.
Nearby sights include the National Park of Saxon Switzerland, the Ore Mountains and Moritzburg Castle.
